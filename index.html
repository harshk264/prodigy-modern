Risk Management Strategy

We believe that successful investing is all about managing risk. Having survived multiple market crashes, we understand that risk management and capital protection are the primary considerations when it comes to making an investment decision. Our survival through the years is down to our strict risk management process.

Risk is mitigated though position sizing, in which we take into account overall market health, valuation changes, scrip performance and crucially, scrip liquidity. We operate with stop losses (price-wise and time-wise), which are amongst the parameters that have been set in place in order to trigger selling. Beyond stop losses at an individual scrip level, we also operate on the basis of a corpus stop loss. With the multiple exit triggers in place, rigorous capital protection is built into our process.

We believe if you manage risk well, the reward will follow!

Prodigy Growth Strategy

Fine-tuned over the last 18 years, Prodigy Growth Strategy aims to deliver long term wealth creation by investing in stocks that we believe are under-researched and/or undervalued and appear to be primed to take advantage of emerging structural change or 'value migration'.

Prodigy Growth Strategy is sector as well as market cap agnostic. We constantly seek out businesses with sufficient liquidity and high quality leadership and management. At the core of our strategy, we look for early alignment with new market leadership, where strong re-rating triggers exist.

Reverse Research

Reverse Research is an unconventional process-driven approach, in which we use our proprietary indicators to define our coverage area for fundamental research. Our indicators are primed to recognise stocks and sectors with a secular change in intrinsic scrip strength, while suppressing 'market noise'.

Once our process picks out various scrips and sectors, we do a deep dive to identify reasons for their strength, carrying out detailed fundamental analysis. Reverse Research has borrowed, in varying degrees, from the investment philosophies of Ed Seykota, Gerald Loeb, Nicholas Darvas and the CANSLIM process of William O'Neil as the foundation of this methodology.

To know more about the Reverse Research process, you can read The Reverse Research Process of Investment Management by our MD & CIO, Ravi Chadha.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Who is eligible for investing in a PMS? ▾
  • Resident Individuals
  • Hindu Undivided Families (HUF)
  • Body Corporate (Private / Public)
  • Registered Trust
  • Non-Resident Indian (subject to RBI approval)
  • Partnership firms or any other eligible investor
2. Is there a minimum investment amount? ▾
  • The minimum amount mandated by SEBI is Rs. 50,00,000 (Rupees Fifty Lakhs) per account. Above the minimum level, you can make additions of any amount to the portfolio.
3. Can the client make partial withdrawals from the portfolio? ▾
  • Partial withdrawals are allowed as along as the withdrawal amount does not cause the value of the portfolio to decrease below the applicable minimum investment amount.
4. How can I monitor the performance of my portfolio? ▾
  • You can login to your portfolio using the portal at the top of this page to check your portfolio any time you want. The portfolio is updated on a T basis.
  • The portfolio statements are emailed to the clients on a monthly basis. Audited statements are provided on an annual basis by 30th June every year.
5. Can I specify investments that I want or don't want to hold? ▾
  • Portfolios are run on a discretionary basis. However, we do accept restrictions in stocks where a specific client has access to price sensitive information / or where the client is restricted from trades due to regulatory requirements. Special requests to exclude certain investments from the portfolio, may be accommodated in certain cases only.
6. Does Prodigy trade in Derivatives (Futures and Options) for the purpose of portfolio hedging. ▾
  • As of date, we have not done so and do not intend to do so. However, this may change in future.
7. What is the difference between a Discretionary PMS and a Non-Discretionary PMS? Does Prodigy run a Non-Discretionary PMS as well? ▾
  • In a discretionary PMS, the portfolio manager makes investment decisions on the client's behalf.
  • In a non-discretionary PMS, the portfolio manager suggests investment ideas to the client. The choice and timing for investment rests with the client while the execution of the investment decision is done by the portfolio manager. Prodigy offers Non-Discretionary PMS on a select basis only.
8. Can one transfer shares to open a PMS account? ▾
  • Yes, you can transfer shares to us as long as the value of the securities transferred exceeds the SEBI mandated minimum portfolio value. However, we prefer if clients sell the shares and transfer funds from the same to us.
9. Is there a lock in period and an exit load? ▾
  • There is no lock in period and we do not levy any exit load. However, we expect clients to stay invested with us for a minimum of 5 years in order for our strategy to see itself through the various market cycles, as returns from equity can be lumpy in nature.
10. How safe are my securities in the PMS? ▾
  • Shares and funds are held with a custodian (Axis Bank) and Prodigy only has a Power of Attorney to buy and sell shares on your behalf. The funds and shares move directly between the custodian and the stock exchange.
11. What is the next step in opening a PMS account? ▾
  • Just fill in your details in the Let's Connect section and we will get in touch with you at the earliest.
  • You can also get in touch with us by sending a mail to info@prodigyinvest.com

Why Prodigy May Not Be Right For You

Our investment style and process are significantly different from our peers and may not be suited to investors who are looking for a buy and hold strategy.

Our portfolio turnover is on the higher side given our extreme focus on risk management. While most other large fund houses have low turnover, ~0.2x, ours has been as high as ~2.25x.

There will be times when our processes may not work and during this period of time we may underperform. While we do not levy any entry / exit loads and we do not have any lock-in, we expect clients to be invested with us for a minimum of 5 years for the strategy to perform to its potential and ride out any patches as mentioned above.
